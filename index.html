Picshr

PicShr is an online platform for sharing photos, akin to Flickr. Users can easily upload, view, and comment on photos. Additionally, users can organize their photos into albums and manage them as they wish. To ensure fast loading times, all photos are stored in AWS S3 buckets and linked to Amazon Cloudfront. To develop PicShr, I utilized my personal photo library, which provides a wide range of high-quality images.

Luxbnb

Introducing Luxbnb - my first full-stack solo web application that replicates the popular Airbnb platform. With Luxbnb, users can easily search for luxurious accommodations all around the world, each rented out by welcoming and accommodating hosts just like you. Creating an account on Luxbnb gives users the opportunity to interact with the spots they have visited by leaving reviews of their experiences. My application's user-friendly interface ensures a seamless experience, enabling users to effortlessly navigate through the site and locate their ideal vacation destination.

Gulp

Gulp is a full-stack Yelp clone that allows users to create and manage a business listing. Collaborating with a team, I focused on both front-end and back-end development to create a seamless user experience.

On the back-end, I designed and developed a Flask API that handled user authentication, business listings, and reviews. I implemented a search functionality that allowed users to search for businesses by name, location, and category. Additionally, I built a review system that enabled users to leave feedback and ratings for businesses they had visited.

On the front-end, I worked with React and Redux to create a user-friendly interface that enabled users to interact with the site seamlessly. I utilized CSS to ensure a consistent design across the site.
